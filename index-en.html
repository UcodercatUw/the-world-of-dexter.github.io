Television Legend

"Dexter" — A revolutionary psychological thriller television series that kept millions of viewers worldwide on edge for eight years. The unique plot about a serial killer who kills other killers challenged traditional notions of good and evil, heroes and antiheroes.

Based on Jeff Lindsay's novels, the series became one of the most discussed television projects of the 2000s, earning critical acclaim and numerous awards, including a Golden Globe.

Main Characters

Dexter Morgan

Main character, Miami Police Department forensic analyst, living a double life as a serial killer who eliminates criminals who have escaped justice.

Michael C. Hall
Debra Morgan

Dexter's sister, Miami Police Department detective. Ambitious, emotional, and completely dedicated to her work, unaware of her brother's dark side.

Jennifer Carpenter
Harry Morgan

Cop, Dexter's adoptive father, who directed his madness into a specific channel, formalized as the "Code of Harry."

James Remar
Angel Batista

Homicide detective working alongside Dexter and Debra. Honest, married, and unhappy in his marriage.

David Zayas
Vince Masuka

Forensic expert, Dexter's colleague. Enjoys crude jokes that don't find appreciation among those around him, but takes his work and research extremely seriously.

C. S. Lee
James Doakes

Homicide sergeant, old friend of LaGuerta. The only cop who openly disliked and distrusted Dexter.

Erik King
Maria LaGuerta

Homicide captain, leading many murder investigations. A good diplomat, actively using media and connections in the Cuban community for career advancement.

Loren Velez
Rita Bennett

Dexter's girlfriend and later wife. Kind, caring, and gentle, plays a key role in his personal life and helps reveal his human side.

Julie Benz

Antagonists

Brian Moser

Serial killer, better known as the "The Ice Truck Killer," who leaves bloodless, dismembered bodies. Under the pseudonym Rudy Cooper, he works as a highly qualified prosthetics specialist. He is Dexter's biological older brother, whose goal was to convince him to join in committing serial murders.

Christian Camargo
Lila West

Artist and Dexter's sponsor in Narcotics Anonymous groups. At first she seems like just an eccentric lover, but soon reveals herself as a dangerous psychopath-pyromaniac, obsessed with Dexter, ready to kill anyone who stands in her way.

Jamie Murray
Miguel Prado

Assistant district attorney who learns Dexter's secret and first becomes his "friend" and student, but soon loses control, kills for personal gain, and manipulates Dexter, becoming a dangerous enemy.

Jimmy Smits
Arthur Mitchell

Serial killer, better known as the "The Trinity Killer," a model family man and church deacon who for 30 years has gotten away with committing ritual murders in cycles of three (later revealed to be four) victims across the country.

John Lithgow
Jordan Chase

Motivational speaker who secretly leads a gang of rapists responsible for serial rapes and murders of young women. Real name - Eugene Greer. Chase rarely gets his hands dirty himself, instead acting as the "brain center" and manipulator, pushing his followers (childhood friends) to commit violent crimes.

Jonny Lee Miller
Travis Marshall

Serial killer, better known as the "The Doomsday Killer," was a member of the Miami Cultural Center who began committing several Bible-related murders to bring about an apocalypse. He had a second personality based on Professor James Gellar, whom he had previously killed.

Colin Hanks
Isaak Sirko

Leader of the Ukrainian mafia "Koshka Brotherhood," who also owns a network of strip clubs, including one in Miami called "Fox Hole." He comes to Miami not to hunt random victims, but to avenge the death of Victor Baskov, his associate (and close person).

Ray Stevenson
Oliver Saxon

Serial killer, better known as the "Brain Surgeon," who removes parts of victims' brains. Real name - Daniel Vogel. Saxon serves as Dexter's evil doppelgänger - another "creation" of Dr. Vogel, but without any constraints or moral code. He took the name "Oliver Saxon" to hide from his past after faking his death in a psychiatric hospital fire.

Darri Ingolfsson

Awards and Recognition

  • • The series "Dexter" was nominated for 7 Golden Globe Awards, winning 2.
  • • "Dexter" was nominated for 11 Emmy Awards and won 4, including 2 in the "Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series" category (Michael C. Hall).
  • • 2010 Screen Actors Guild Award: Outstanding Male Actor

Book Series

The series is based on Jeff Lindsay's novels:

  1. Darkly Dreaming Dexter (2004)
  2. Dearly Devoted Dexter (2005)
  3. Dexter in the Dark (2007)
  4. Dexter by Design (2009)
  5. Dexter is Delicious (2010)
  6. Double Dexter (2011)
  7. Dexter's Final Cut (2013)
  8. Dexter is Dead (2015)

Cultural Impact

"Dexter" had a significant impact on popular culture, television, and public perception of morality. It became one of the pillars of television's "golden age," when the boundaries between good and evil in main characters were completely blurred.
