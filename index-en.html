Television legend

"Dexter" — A revolutionary psychological thriller television series, which gripped millions of viewers worldwide for eight years. The unique plot of a serial killer who kills other killers challenged traditional notions of good and evil, heroes and antiheroes.

The series, based on the novels by Jeff Lindsay, became one of the most talked-about television projects of the 2000s, receiving critical acclaim and numerous awards, including a Golden Globe Award.

The protagonist, a Miami police forensic scientist, leads a double life as a serial killer, who eliminates criminals who have escaped justice.

Antagonists

Christian Camargo
Lila West

An artist and Dexter's sponsor in Narcotics Anonymous. At first, she appears to be just an eccentric lover, but soon reveals herself to be a dangerous, psychotic, pyromaniac, obsessed with Dexter, ready to kill anyone who gets in her way.

Awards and recognition

  • • The series "Dexter" was nominated for seven Golden Globe Awards, winning two.
  • • Dexter was nominated for 11 Emmy Awards and won four, including two for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series (Michael C. Hall).
  • • 2010 Screen Actors Guild Award: Best Actor

Book series

The series is based on the novels by Jeff Lindsay:

  1. Dexter's Dreaming Demon (2004)
  2. Dexter's Good Friend (2005)
  3. Dexter in the Dark (2007)
  4. Dexter in Action (2009)
  5. Dexter's Delicacies (2010)
  6. Dexter's Double (2011)
  7. Dexter's Final Take (2013)
  8. Dexter is Dead (2015)

Cultural influence

"Dexter" had a significant impact on popular culture, television, and public perceptions of morality. It became one of the pillars of television's "golden age," when the lines between good and evil in its main characters became completely blurred.
